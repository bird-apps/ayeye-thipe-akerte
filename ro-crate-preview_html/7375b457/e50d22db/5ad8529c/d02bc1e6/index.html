artetyerretyerr

Willy-wagtail
Rhipidura leucophrys

Thipe artetyerretyerre unte aretyeke, urrperle, tayele arlpentye, ante inwenge mperlkere. Renhe angkerlenge awerle, akngerre anthurre. Re apeke ngkwenge ileme, arne iwenhe apeke renhe aremele. Apmwe apeke aremele, aw unte apeke apmere kwenele aneme, re ilelheme rarle akethele anemele. Re arlkweme intelyapelyape akweke, tyape aparre ahelhe-arenye, ante alkereke-irrentye akngerre itne arlwentye akngerre mape. Antywe ikwerenhe re mpwareme arneke arlpare anetyeke yakwethe akweke arteke, kwarte ikwerenhe arlke. Anwerne artetyeretyerre arlkwentye akngerre kwenye.

You see the Willy Wagtail, it is black in colour, long tail and white breast. When they talk they talk real loud. It tells you about anything that it sees. Maybe it sees a snake, or if you are inside the house he's telling you he's outside there. He eats small butterflies, grubs that live in the ground and any kind of insects that fly around. It makes its nest, it looks like a little bag hanging in the tree, for its eggs. We would never eat the willy wagtail bird.
