lyerre-lyerre

wren
Malurus lamberti

Thipe lyerre-lyerre apele thipe akweke anthurre mape. Putye-werne alhemele you arerle arne akertneke atningke anthurre angkerremele anerrirrerlenge. Alkereke imarte irremele apurte akwete arne arrpenhe-werne anteme unterlenge. Ntange itne ahelheke kutyemele arlkweme, merne itne-kenhe. Kwatyeke irrperreme, antywe itne mpwareme arne akertneke. Utnanthe ulteke-arleke aw antywe itne arneke-arleke, artetyerretyerr-kenhe- arteke akine. Ante anwerne lyerre-lyerre arlkwentye akngerre kwenye, kere anetyakenhe.

The variegated fairy wren is one of the small birds. When you go bush you see them up in the trees, a big mob of them all talking. Then they fly away in a group from one tree to another. They eat grass seeds from the ground, that's their food. They gather near water, and they make nests up in the trees. They make their nests in the sides of the bushes, similar to the willy wagtail. We never eat wrens, they are not meat for us.
